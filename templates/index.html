When do morels usually pop?

Most areas see morels in spring as soils warm. A practical rule of thumb is ~45–55°F soil temperature plus moisture and a warming trend.

What trees are “morel magnets”?

Morels are frequently found near elm, ash, apple, and poplar. Local species and habitat matter—edges, disturbed ground, and old tree lines can be excellent.

Does rain matter?

Yes. Moisture helps trigger fruiting, especially after warm days. This tool looks at the last week of weather as a fast indicator—not a guarantee.

“Record board” style facts (high-confidence, non-fake)
  • Most reliable “big haul” factor: being in the right habitat during a warming trend right after rain.
  • Most common pattern: south-facing slopes earlier in spring; north-facing slopes later.
  • Most repeated field advice: walk slow—morels blend into leaf litter until your eyes “tune in.”
  • Most legendary moment: you find one… then realize you’re standing in a patch.
