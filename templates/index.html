FAQ + Field Notes

Best “quick indicator” for morel timing?

A combo of soil warming (roughly 45–55°F) + moisture + a warming trend. This app checks recent weather to estimate that window.

Where do morels like to hide?

Look in mature hardwood areas with good leaf litter: edges of old woods, creek bottoms, and places that hold moisture. Many hunters check around elm, ash, apple, and poplar.

How should I “walk a patch”?

Slow down. Scan for the honeycomb cap texture and subtle tan/gray tones. When you find one, stop and check a 10–15 foot circle — patches are real.

Forager checklist (what people actually forget)
  • Mesh bag (spores drop as you walk)
  • Small brush or soft paintbrush
  • Tick protection + a mirror check in the car
  • Mark your car location (woods look different on the way back)
  • Water + a snack (low blood sugar makes you miss mushrooms)
Safety note (real talk)

Always positively identify mushrooms before eating. “False morels” exist and can be dangerous. If you’re not 100% sure, get confirmation from a trusted local mycology group.
